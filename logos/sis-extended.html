Cormorant SC 300. Small-caps variant of Cormorant. The S is slightly wider and squarer — more monumental. The same hairline contrast but with a more upright, architectural quality. Best for institutional signage.

Lora 400. Calligraphic transitional serif — similar contrast to Cormorant but slightly more robust and contemporary. Warmer and more approachable. Excellent screen rendering at all sizes.

Marcellus. Roman lapidary inscriptions — upright, clean, precise. Less calligraphic than Cormorant but shares the high stroke contrast. The S is more architectural and geometric. Signals rigour.

Cinzel 400. Based on Roman stone inscriptions — extreme stroke contrast, flat serifs, monumental authority. The S is wider and more heroic. Closest to a space-agency wordmark feel. Very distinctive.

Libre Caslon Display. Classic Anglo-American typeface with generous ink traps and robust serifs. The S is wider and more traditional than Cormorant. Familiar, trustworthy — editorial gravitas of a scientific journal.

Della Respira. Roman inscriptional serifs with a warm, humanist rhythm. The S has flowing curves without feeling calligraphic. Dignified and refined — less common than Cormorant, giving the mark a unique voice.
A9Forum

Forum. Roman epigraphic with uncial roots. Upright, monumental, slightly informal — the S has a distinctive open curve and clean exit strokes. Unusual choice that reads as both ancient and contemporary.
