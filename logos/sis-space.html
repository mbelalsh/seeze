Conjunction geometry. Two spacecraft trajectories crossing — one solid, one dashed — with probability cones showing forecast uncertainty. The red close-approach dot is the conjunction event itself. This is literally what SiS does: predicts whether two objects will collide, and how certain that prediction is. Most technically accurate to the product.

3 uncertainty cone
S S
Safe · Interpretable · Simple
Uncertainty propagation cone. 1σ/2σ/3σ probability bands fanning from a single origin — the canonical way uncertainty in trajectory forecasting is visualised. Any orbital mechanic or AI researcher recognises this immediately. More technical than the Earth limb. Better for academic contexts (AFRL white papers, conference slides) than consumer-facing use.
