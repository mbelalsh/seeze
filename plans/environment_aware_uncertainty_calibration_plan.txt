
ENVIRONMENT-AWARE UNCERTAINTY CALIBRATION FOR CONJUNCTION ASSESSMENT
Strategic Research and Startup Development Plan

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Core Idea
------------------------------------------------------------

The central concept is to model how environmental disturbances affect orbital
uncertainty and then use CTPC (Continuous-Time Probabilistic Corrector) to
recalibrate the resulting covariance.

Conceptual pipeline:

Sensors
↓
Orbit Determination
↓
Deterministic Propagation (GMAT / physics model)
↓
Environmental Drivers
(space weather, drag variability)
↓
Uncertainty Distortion
↓
CTPC Correction
↓
Calibrated Covariance
↓
Collision Probability (Pc) Computation
↓
Risk-Aware Maneuver Planning
↓
Operator Decision

Key hypothesis:
Environmental disturbances distort orbital uncertainty. CTPC can learn to
recalibrate this distorted uncertainty to produce stable collision probability
estimates.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Main Research Question
------------------------------------------------------------

How do environmental disturbances affect the evolution of orbital uncertainty,
and how can learned correctors recalibrate the resulting covariance to maintain
statistical consistency for conjunction assessment?

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Environmental Drivers to Model
------------------------------------------------------------

Focus on disturbances that directly influence orbital prediction errors.

Primary factors:

• Solar activity (F10.7 solar flux)
• Geomagnetic storms (Ap / Kp index)
• Thermospheric density variability
• Atmospheric drag fluctuations
• Density model errors (NRLMSISE-00, JB2008)

These environmental variables primarily affect Low Earth Orbit satellites by
changing atmospheric drag.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Research Direction 1: Space-Weather-Driven Drag Uncertainty
------------------------------------------------------------

Objective:
Understand how space weather affects drag uncertainty and propagation error.

Approach:

1. Collect solar activity indices:
   - F10.7 solar flux
   - Ap geomagnetic index

2. Use atmospheric models:
   - NRLMSISE-00
   - JB2008

3. Simulate orbital propagation under varying solar conditions.

Study how the following quantities change:

• drag acceleration uncertainty
• orbital decay variability
• covariance growth

Deliverable:

Quantitative model of how space weather amplifies orbital prediction uncertainty.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Research Direction 2: Environment-Conditioned CTPC
------------------------------------------------------------

Extend CTPC so that it uses environmental inputs.

Baseline corrector:

corrector(state history)

Environment-aware corrector:

corrector(state history, environment features)

Possible environmental features:

• F10.7 solar flux
• Ap geomagnetic index
• density model residuals
• estimated drag variability

Goal:

Allow CTPC to adapt its uncertainty correction based on environmental conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Research Direction 3: Storm-Aware Covariance Propagation
------------------------------------------------------------

Simulate orbital propagation under three scenarios:

1. Quiet solar conditions
2. Moderate geomagnetic storm
3. Extreme geomagnetic storm

For each scenario compute uncertainty using:

• STM covariance propagation
• Monte Carlo simulation
• CTPC calibrated covariance

Evaluate:

• Mahalanobis distance consistency
• covariance calibration error
• Pc stability

------------------------------------------------------------
7. Research Direction 4: Environmental Uncertainty Maps
------------------------------------------------------------

Construct maps showing how prediction uncertainty grows under environmental disturbances.

Key variables:

• orbit altitude
• solar activity level
• forecast horizon

Outputs:

uncertainty growth surfaces
covariance inflation factors
risk sensitivity maps

These visualizations could become important figures for publications and the startup website.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. Research Direction 5: Pc Stability Under Environmental Disturbances
------------------------------------------------------------

Simulate conjunction scenarios under:

• quiet space weather
• geomagnetic storms
• density spikes

Measure:

• Pc variance across CDM updates
• alert stability
• maneuver planning consistency

Compare:

baseline propagator Pc
vs
CTPC calibrated Pc

Goal:

Demonstrate that CTPC stabilizes collision risk estimation under environmental disturbances.
