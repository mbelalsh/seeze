
ENVIRONMENT-AWARE UNCERTAINTY CALIBRATION FOR CONJUNCTION ASSESSMENT
3-PAPER + 1-DEMO RESEARCH AND STARTUP ROADMAP

====================================================================
CORE THEME
====================================================================

Environmental disturbances distort orbital uncertainty. SiS calibrates
this uncertainty using CTPC (Continuous-Time Probabilistic Corrector)
to produce stable collision-risk estimates and better maneuver decisions.

System chain:

Observation → Prediction → Uncertainty Calibration → Risk Estimation → Decision Support

This roadmap ensures that:
• the PhD thesis is coherent
• papers are publishable
• the SiS startup narrative is strengthened
• a working demonstrator is produced


====================================================================
PAPER 1 — CHARACTERIZING THE PROBLEM
====================================================================

Working title:
Environmental Disturbances and Uncertainty Miscalibration in Satellite Conjunction Assessment

Purpose:
Demonstrate that environmental disturbances (e.g., space weather effects)
create measurable uncertainty miscalibration in conjunction prediction.

Main question:
How do environmental disturbances degrade covariance calibration and
destabilize collision probability estimates?

Setup:
Simulate conjunction prediction under:

• quiet space weather conditions
• moderate geomagnetic activity
• strong disturbance conditions

Baseline uncertainty methods:

• STM / linearized covariance propagation
• tuned process noise models
• Monte Carlo reference propagation

Key variables:

• forecast horizon
• orbital altitude
• solar activity indices
• drag variability

Metrics:

• squared Mahalanobis consistency
• negative log-likelihood (NLL)
• covariance coverage error
• Pc volatility across updates
• alert inflation under disturbance

Key figures:

• calibration error vs forecast horizon
• Pc instability vs disturbance intensity
• covariance inflation maps
• altitude × solar activity heatmaps

Value:
Establishes the operational problem and motivates the SiS solution.


====================================================================
PAPER 2 — ENVIRONMENT-AWARE CTPC
====================================================================

Working title:
Environment-Conditioned Continuous-Time Probabilistic Correction
for Orbital Uncertainty Calibration

Purpose:
Develop the environment-aware CTPC method.

Main question:
Can environmental signals improve uncertainty correction during disturbed
orbital prediction regimes?

Method extension:

Baseline CTPC:
corrector(state history)

Environment-aware CTPC:
corrector(state history, environment features)

Environment features:

• F10.7 solar flux
• Ap / Kp geomagnetic indices
• density-model residual proxies
• drag variability indicators
• forecast horizon

Model comparisons:

• deterministic propagation
• deterministic + tuned process noise
• CTPC without environment features
• environment-conditioned CTPC

Metrics:

• Mahalanobis consistency
• covariance calibration curves
• probability distribution distance
• Pc stability across updates
• horizon-dependent performance

Expected outcome:

Environment-aware CTPC should outperform standard CTPC particularly
under disturbed conditions.

Value:
This becomes the core technical contribution of the research program.


====================================================================
PAPER 3 — DECISION IMPACT
====================================================================

Working title:
Risk-Aware Maneuver Planning with Environment-Calibrated Uncertainty
for Conjunction Assessment

Purpose:
Show that improved uncertainty calibration leads to better maneuver decisions.

Main question:
Does environment-aware uncertainty calibration improve maneuver planning
quality and safety?

Comparison scenarios:

• baseline uncertainty propagation
• standard CTPC
• environment-aware CTPC

Decision framework:

Constrained maneuver optimization:

objective: minimize fuel / Δv
constraint: Pc below safety threshold

Optional risk-aware formulation:
robust constraint or CVaR-based safety margin

Metrics:

• violation rate (true Pc after maneuver)
• fuel optimality gap
• false-safe recommendation rate
• conservatism gap
• maneuver stability across updates
• alert-to-action decision quality

Expected result:

Environment-aware calibration produces safer and more stable maneuver
recommendations.

Value:
Transforms uncertainty calibration into operational decision support.

DEMO — SIS CONJUNCTION DECISION ENGINE
====================================================================

Title:
Environment-Aware Conjunction Decision Demonstrator

Purpose:
Show the complete SiS system working end-to-end.

Demo scenario:

Two satellites in a conjunction event under varying environmental conditions.

System pipeline:

1. Orbit propagation
2. Environmental disturbance input
3. CTPC uncertainty calibration
4. Collision probability estimation
5. Risk-aware maneuver recommendation
6. Operator decision output

Interface layout:

Panel 1 — Orbital visualization
• satellite tracks
• uncertainty ellipsoids
• time-of-closest-approach indicator

Panel 2 — Risk metrics
• baseline Pc
• calibrated Pc
• disturbance condition indicator

Panel 3 — Maneuver planning
• recommended maneuver time
• Δv requirement
• expected Pc after maneuver
• robustness margin

Key demonstration scenario:

Quiet vs geomagnetic storm conditions.

Expected visualization:

• baseline Pc fluctuates heavily
• CTPC-calibrated Pc stabilizes
• maneuver recommendation becomes consistent
