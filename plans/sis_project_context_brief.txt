Researcher Background

I am a PhD student working on uncertainty-aware dynamical systems modeling, particularly for spacecraft trajectory forecasting and conjunction assessment.

My work focuses on Predictor–Corrector frameworks where:
- a physics-based propagator predicts system dynamics
- a learned corrector calibrates prediction errors and uncertainty

A key method I am developing is:

CTPC (Continuous-Time Probabilistic Corrector)

This model learns to correct uncertainty evolution in dynamical systems.

Core Technical Idea

Most space traffic systems rely on deterministic orbit propagation with simple uncertainty assumptions.

However, real operational conditions include:

- thermospheric density variability
- atmospheric drag uncertainty
- solar activity effects
- geomagnetic disturbances

These environmental factors distort orbital prediction uncertainty.

The key problem is:

uncertainty miscalibration in orbit prediction.

This leads to:

- unstable collision probability estimates
- alert thrashing
- conservative or unnecessary maneuvers
